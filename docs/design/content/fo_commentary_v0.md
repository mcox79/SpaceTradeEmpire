| Code | Name | Voice | Arc |
|------|------|-------|-----|
| ANA | Maren (Analyst) | Data-driven, clinical, probability-focused. Speaks in percentages and models. | Starts clinical, gradually reveals vulnerability beneath the numbers. |
| VET | Dask (Veteran) | Threat-assessment, tactical, duty-bound. Speaks from experience. | Starts as perimeter-first tactician, gradually discovers what peace feels like. |

### T3.01 -- First Dock at Haven

**ANA.T3.01**:

Station log, personal annotation. Haven initial assessment.

Power-up sequence completed in 4.7 minutes. That's faster than any cold-start procedure in my training manuals. The accommodation geometry appears to operate on principles I can describe mathematically but cannot derive from first principles. I have fourteen pages of sensor readings and none of them explain why the air recyclers started before we connected to external power.

Efficiency metrics: breathable atmosphere within 6 minutes. Docking clamp alignment: autonomous, sub-millimeter precision. Station temperature: 18.4 degrees, which is biologically optimal for our species, despite this station having no record of our species ever visiting.

I am not comfortable with the implications of that last observation. Logging it without interpretation. For now.

**VET.T3.01**:

Haven. First night.

I've done perimeter sweeps of six stations in my career. This is the first one where nothing was wrong. No blind spots in the sensor coverage. No structural weak points in the docking ring. No gaps in the defensive geometry. Whoever built this place designed it so that defending it would be trivial, and I don't know if that comforts me or terrifies me.

I set up a watch rotation out of habit. There's nobody to stand the second shift. The Keeper -- whatever it is -- brightened the corridor lights when I walked the perimeter. Like it was trying to help.

I've slept in a lot of stations. This is the first one where the silence doesn't feel like something's missing. It feels like something's enough.

**PTH.T3.01**:

It's real.

All those anomalous readings, all those shimmer-boundary signatures that didn't match any known phenomenon -- they were pointing here. To this. A station that exists in stable space without containment infrastructure. That should be impossible and it's sitting here, humming gently, like it's been waiting.

The walls have patterns. Not decorative -- functional. I traced one line from the docking ring to what I think is a resonance chamber, and the pattern never repeated. Not once in 200 meters. Quasi-crystalline. Self-similar but never periodic. My fingers were shaking and it wasn't from the cold.

I've been searching for proof that accommodation geometry is real for my entire career. It's in the walls. It's under my feet. It's in the air I'm breathing. I need to sit down.

### T3.02 -- First Ancient Log Discovered

**ANA.T3.02**:

Personal log. First data log recovered from Haven archive.

The format is unfamiliar but the structure is recognizable. Header, metadata, conversational transcript. Someone recorded a discussion between colleagues and filed it in a database. That's... that's what I would do. That's what anyone would do.
