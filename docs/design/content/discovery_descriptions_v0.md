### Chain A — The Valorin Expedition (3 sites)

**Register**: Frontier tension → displacement evidence → containment revelation

**A.1 — Expedition Staging Post** (Hook — Derelict)
*Scan*: Cluster of supply caches, Valorin design, but older than any Valorin expansion record. Cache integrity: 97%. Contents: standard frontier equipment — rations, navigation markers, hull sealant. One anomaly: the navigation markers reference systems that don't appear on any Valorin chart.

*Analysis (Maren)*: "These caches predate the Valorin Compact by at least three generations. Someone ran a frontier expedition using Valorin equipment before the Valorin organized into clans. The navigation markers point to systems beyond the current Shelf boundary — systems that were accessible then but are not now. The frontier was larger."

**A.2 — Weapons Test Range** (Development — Ruin)
*Scan*: Blast-scarred asteroid field. Weapon signatures do not match any modern faction armament. Scoring patterns indicate directed-energy weapons of extraordinary precision — surgical cuts in rock, each one identical. This was not combat. It was calibration.

*Analysis (Dask)*: "Someone tested weapons here. Not to destroy — to measure. Each blast scar is exactly 2.3 meters deep, 0.8 meters wide. Hundreds of them, all identical. They were dialing in a weapon system with precision that makes our targeting look like guesswork. And then they stopped. The last test scar is unfinished — half-depth, as if they were interrupted."

**A.3 — Defense Array Fragment** (Climax — Ruin)
*Scan*: Massive structural remnant — a single component of a larger system. Cross-referencing with Valorin Shelf data: this fragment's resonance signature matches the Shelf boundary. This was part of the wall. Not a wall built to keep things out. A wall built to keep things in.

*Analysis (Lira)*: "We've been thinking about the Shelf as a boundary — the edge of habitable space. But this fragment is oriented inward. Its weapon systems, its sensor arrays, its structural reinforcement — all pointing at us. The Shelf isn't a frontier. It's a perimeter. And whatever built it was not worried about what's outside.
