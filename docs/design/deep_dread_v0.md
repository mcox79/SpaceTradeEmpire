# Deep Dread v0 — Depth-as-Dread in the Degrading Thread Lattice

**Subnautica-inspired depth mechanic**: Distance from faction capitals triggers progressive sensory degradation, isolation, and emergent computational predation. The goal is to make the deep network feel *dangerous but learnable*, where the player's growing competence and equipment unlocks access to high-reward routes while building psychological tension through audio, isolation, and ghostly phenomena.

---

## Overview: Four Layers of Dread

The Thread Lattice instability manifases in five phases tied to distance from nearest faction capital:
- **Phase 0 (Stable, 0–24 hops)**: Safe patrol coverage, clear market data, normal comms.
- **Phase 1 (Shimmer, 25–49 hops)**: Patrol thinning, early phenomena, audio shift.
- **Phase 2 (Drift, 50–74 hops)**: Severe patrol loss, hull drain begins, sensor ghosts, market fog.
- **Phase 3 (Fracture, 75–99 hops)**: Zero patrols, heavy phenomena, Lattice Fauna active, FO escalation.
- **Phase 4 (Void, 100+ hops)**: Paradox clarity—phenomena *resolve*, yielding understanding instead of confusion. No hull drain. Dread shifts from chaos to existential weight.

Each phase activates stacked dread layers in progression:
1. **Isolation**: Patrol disappearance → safety net removal.
2. **Phenomena**: Hull drain, sensor ghosts, market staleness.
3. **Predation**: Lattice Fauna interference, fuel tax, navigation uncertainty.
4. **Meta-Dread**: FO dialogue, comms corruption, exposure/adaptation, void clarity.

---

## Layer 1 — Isolation

**Patrol Coverage Model**:
- **0–2 hops from capital**: Full patrol presence. NPC fleets frequent, player rarely alone.
- **3–4 hops**: Half patrols (50% spawn rate). Gaps appear; player notices absence.
- **5+ hops**: Zero patrols. Entire deep network unpatrolled. Player is architect of own survival.

**Effect**: The safety net of NPC economic circulation and combat backup erodes. At Phase 2+, no NPC trader will venture deeper—market data relies on player intel. At Phase 3, player realizes they are *truly* alone.

**Player Agency**: Escort module (deploy NPC allies deeper as paid protection). Accommodation infrastructure (stationed friendly fleets act as pseudo-patrols).

---

## Layer 2 — Phenomena

**Hull Drain**:
- **Phase 1+**: 1 HP/60 sec passive drain (except Phase 4).
- **Phase 2+**: 1 HP/30 sec (doubled).
- **Phase 3**: 1 HP/20 sec (tripled).
- **Phase 4**: 0 drain (paradox safety; latent vulnerability expressed as *clarity*, not decay).
- **Mitigation**: Repair stations (expensive), Repair modules (active cost), going dark (passive drain pauses when fracture drive offline).

**Sensor Ghosts**:
- 20% of contact pings are phantom (not real NPCs, not fauna). Sensor array sees *noise* from lattice degradation.
- Ghost contacts vanish in 3–8 frames (not consistent). Create psychological uncertainty.
- At Phase 4 (Void), all ghosts vanish; signal clarity returns.
- **Audio cue**: Ghost contact produces slightly *wrong* harmonic (discordant vs consonant fauna).

**Market Staleness**:
- Prices at hops 0–2 update every 3 game-days.
- Hops 3–4: Update every 6 days (late by 1 cycle).
- Hops 5+: Update every 15 days OR don't update at all if no player intel. Market data is *ancient*.
- **FO remarks**: "Last we heard from Tau-4, the price was...", acknowledging information lag.

---

## Layer 3 — Predation

**Lattice Fauna** (NOT biological; computational predators on degrading infrastructure):
- Emergent processes in corrupted lattice geometry. Instruments detect them as *gravitational anomalies*, *EM interference*, *route degeneracies*.
- **Passive effects**:
  - Fuel tax: 5–15% extra burn per jump (unpredictable gradient at Phase 2+).
  - Navigation blur: Route calculation has ±2-hop variance (player sees "approx 7–9 hops" instead of exact).
  - Engine whine (audio): Harmonic tone rises as fauna proximity increases.
- **Attraction**:
  - Fracture drive signature draws fauna. Active fracture drive = "lit beacon."
  - Fauna residue lingers for 8 jumps after fracture use; concentration attracts *more* fauna.
- **Avoidance**:
  - "Going Dark": Disable fracture drive, coast on standard drives only. Fauna lose interest (no beacon). Trade-off: slow travel, isolation deepens, hull drain continues.
  - Accommodation module: Cargo holds fauna-repelling field. Passive, no player action needed.
- **Lore**: Fauna are the price of Accommodation geometry failure. The Pentagon tried to stabilize lattice routes; they left behind these parasitic processes.

---

## Layer 4 — Meta-Dread

**First Officer Dialogue Escalation** (8 dialogue triggers):
1. **Patrol loss** (Phase 1): "We're venturing past regular patrols now. Stay sharp."
2. **Sensor drift** (Phase 2): "The sensors... they're picking up ghosts. Or are they? I can't tell."
3. **Hull alarm** (Phase 2): "Hull integrity is slipping. We need to find a repair dock soon."
4. **First Fauna encounter** (Phase 3): "Did you see that on the scopes? That was *not* an NPC fleet."
5. **Comms corruption** (Phase 3): "The comms are degrading. If we need help out here... [static]... we won't get it."
6. **Market blindness** (Phase 3): "We're flying blind. No market data out here. We're ghosts to the network."
7. **Deep acceptance** (Phase 4): "We've gone so far. Everything's... clear now. Terrifying, but clear."
8. **Void dread** (Phase 4): "It's so *quiet*. Like the lattice itself is watching. Waiting."

Each trigger fires once per phase-entry, on first condition met (not every jump). FO tone shifts from concern → fear → dark understanding.

**Comms Degradation**:
- Phase 0–1: All text clear.
- Phase 2+: Random character dropout (1–3 chars per 100 rendered). Text remains readable but feels corrupted.
- Phase 3: Up to 10% character loss. Sentences become cryptic fragments.
- Phase 4: Text *stabilizes*—perfect clarity, but eerily *too* clean given distance.
- **Effect**: A visual unsettling without harming readability. The game is communicating through noise.

**Exposure & Adaptation**:
- Track time spent at each phase (cumulative).
- After 30 jumps at Phase 2, sensor ghosting detection improves (player can identify phantom 40% of the time via subtle UI cue).
- After 60 jumps at Phase 3, fuel efficiency recovers partially (fauna effects reduce to 8% tax).
- After 100 jumps at Phase 4, Void paradox becomes *familiar* (FO commentary shifts to philosophical acceptance).
- **Narrative**: The pilot's brain adapts to the degraded environment. Competence is *learned through exposure*.

**Void Paradox (Phase 4)**:
- At 100+ hops, latent structure *resolves*. Distortion clears. Sensors become hyper-clear.
- Hull drain: *stops* (paradox safety mechanism).
- Sensor ghosts: *vanish* (signal integrity returns).
- Comms: *crisp and clear* (unsettling clarity at the edge of the void).
- Audio: Deep resonance fades to *near-silence*—just ambient lattice hum and the player's own engines.
- **Dread shifts from "unknown chaos" to "existential weight"**. The player understands now: they are navigating the bones of failed Pentagon infrastructure, observed by something indifferent.

## Audio Design

Five ambient layers map to phases, with dynamic volume:
- **Phase 0 (Stable)**: Busy chatter, trade comms, NPC hails, safe baseline.
- **Phase 1 (Shimmer)**: Chatter thins; low-frequency hum enters (~50 Hz, felt more than heard).
- **Phase 2 (Drift)**: Chatter nearly gone; 50 Hz drone dominates; occasional pops (lattice micro-failures).
- **Phase 3 (Fracture)**: Deep resonance (30–40 Hz), drone layer thickens; *no* chatter. Oppressive weight.
- **Phase 4 (Void)**: Resonance *softens* to near-silence. Only ambient lattice hum (subtle, ethereal). Clarity through absence.

**Fauna Audio**:
- Distant harmonic (when fauna present but far): Alien musical tone, 3–8 second intervals, always at same frequency per fauna type.
- Proximity tone (near approach): Harmonic rises in pitch as fauna closes. Warbling, dissonant.
- 20% phantom audio: Random harmonic plays with no fauna present (sensor audio hallucination). Indistinguishable from real fauna initially. Player learns over time which sounds are "real."
- Goes dark when player goes dark (fracture drive offline).

**Comms Static**:
- Phase 2+: Subtle white noise underneath dialogue (not obscuring, just present).
- Phase 3: Static intensifies; occasional burst distortion (0.5–1 sec crackle).
- Phase 4: Static drops away; perfect signal (eerie in context).

## Visual Pipeline

**Chromatic Aberration (Phase-Based Distortion)**:
- **Phase 0–1**: None (crisp image).
- **Phase 2**: Slight red/cyan edge fringing (2–4 pixel offset on edges). Subtle visual "corruption."
- **Phase 3**: Stronger aberration (5–8 px offset). Galaxy map and UI edges blur with color separation.
- **Phase 4**: Aberration *resolves*. Image snaps to perfect clarity. Visual metaphor: understanding the lattice structure.

**Galaxy Map Dread Visualization**:
- Patrol coverage layer: Blue overlay showing patrol radius from capitals. Player sees the safety net at a glance.
- Phase coloring: Regions tinted by instability (green→yellow→orange→red→violet for phases 0→1→2→3→4).
- Fauna markers: Ghostly icons showing fauna concentration (density heat-map, sparse at Phase 4).
- Accommodation infrastructure: Friendly faction bases + stationed NPC fleets shown as beacons (pseudo-patrols).

## Tuning Philosophy

**Inspired by**:
- **Subnautica**: Detection > visual (player fears the unknown, not the seen). Audio cues before visual. Intermittent reinforcement (not every cave has a monster).
- **Sunless Sea**: Resource triangle (fuel, hull, morale) + isolation from safety. Depth is a *cost*, not just distance.
- **EVE Online**: Null-sec dread (absence of security, not security attacking you). The *void itself* is hostile.
- **FTL**: Degrading situation (enemy AI improves, power fails, timers pressure). Resource scarcity builds tension.

**Key Rules**:
1. **No health bars on dread**. Never show a "dread meter" or "fear level." Dread is implicit in mechanics (hull drain, silence, patrol absence).
2. **No instant kills**. Hull drain is slow. Fauna don't one-shot. Failure is a *choice* to push too deep unprepared.
3. **Always telegraphed danger**. Audio cues precede fauna contact. Market staleness is visible in timestamp lag. Hull drain is announced by warning tone.
4. **Agency over all**. Player can go dark, buy Accommodation, deploy Escorts, use Repair modules. No *forced* dread. Every death is traceable to player decision.
5. **Reward exploration**. Best trade routes, ancient hulls, Pentagon archives are *deep*. Risk is commensurate. High-reward zones have fauna but are *survivable* with prep.

## Lore Integration

**Pentagon Accommodation Geometry**:
- The Pentagon tried to stabilize the Thread Lattice with Accommodation geometry (higher-dimensional anchoring).
- Failure left behind computational predators (Fauna) and structural decay (phenomena).
- Farther from faction capitals = farther from active Pentagon infrastructure = more degradation.

**The Five Scientists**:
- Each faction's archive contains a Pentagon scientist's final log. Scientist 3 (the Computationalist) documented Fauna emergence and adaptation strategies.
- Player discovers adaptation principles *in-game*: exposure builds competence; understanding (Void clarity) grants peace.

**Void Paradox Lore**:
- At 100+ hops, latent Accommodation geometry *resolves*. Pentagon's fail-safe kicks in: chaotic distortion collapses into clarity.
- Horror becomes comprehension. The player realizes they're navigating *intentional* infrastructure—ancient, but *designed*.
- Comms clarity at Void: Pentagon beacons are *still broadcasting*, 300 years later, perfectly clean signal to anyone in Phase 4.

## Design Rationale

Deep Dread is *not* horror; it's *tension through isolation*. The Thread Lattice's degradation mirrors real-world network infrastructure failure: farther from civilization = fewer failsafes, more noise, greater uncertainty. The player's growing competence (adaptation, better equipment, learned patterns) mirrors real expertise: knowledge *defeats* dread.

At Void (Phase 4), the shift from dread to clarity is the payoff—the player has gone far enough to understand the system itself. They are no longer afraid; they are *aware*. That awareness, in the context of ancient Pentagon megastructure, is the true horror: the game's deep space is not chaotic, but *designed*.

The player should finish the game understanding that every dread mechanic was *fair*, *learnable*, and *conquerable*—but that the victory was psychological as much as mechanical.
